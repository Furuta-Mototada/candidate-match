🗳️ Candidate Match

AI-Powered Political Matching for the Japanese National Diet
Mototada Furuta · Minerva University Capstone
"What if finding the politician who best represents you was as easy as answering a few questions?"

The Problem

Japan's National Diet passes hundreds of bills each session, yet most voters have little insight into how their representatives actually vote. Traditional media focuses on party labels and personalities rather than legislative behavior — leaving citizens unable to determine which politicians truly share their views.

The Solution

Candidate Match is a web application that uses Computerized Adaptive Testing (CAT) to help anyone find their closest match in parliament. Users answer a short series of questions about real bills — "Do you support or oppose this legislation?" — and the system uses machine learning to find Diet members whose voting records are most similar.

Figure 1 — The user journey: from sign-up to personalized match results.

Impact

By making legislative data accessible and personal, Candidate Match aims to strengthen democratic participation in Japan — helping voters engage with politics based on policy, not just party affiliation. The long-term vision includes expanding to local assemblies and supporting civic-education programs for young voters.
