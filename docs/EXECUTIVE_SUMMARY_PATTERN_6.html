❌ The Problem: Voters in the Dark

Japan's National Diet passes hundreds of bills each session, yet most voters have no idea how their representatives actually vote. Traditional media focuses on party labels, scandals, and personalities — never on the specific legislation that shapes everyday life.

Without clear, accessible data, citizens cannot determine which politicians truly share their views. The result? Many voters simply follow party loyalty — or don't vote at all.

⚙️ How It Works: Adaptive Intelligence

Candidate Match uses Computerized Adaptive Testing (CAT) — the same technique behind modern standardized tests. The system selects the most informative bill to ask about next, so you get accurate matches with fewer questions.

Behind the scenes, bill text is converted into mathematical embeddings, parliament members are mapped in a latent space using SVD, and similar bills are grouped by K-Means clustering. An optional OpenAI integration provides plain-language bill summaries.
